<p><p>The meat testing market comprises two main types: traditional testing and rapid testing. Traditional testing involves established laboratory methods, including culturing techniques and biochemical assays, ensuring thorough detection of pathogens and contaminants, but often requiring longer turnaround times. In contrast, rapid testing utilizes innovative technologies, such as polymerase chain reaction (PCR) and immunoassays, enabling quicker results and on-site testing. This efficiency addresses food safety concerns promptly, making rapid testing increasingly popular among producers and regulators seeking immediate insights into meat quality and safety.</p></p>
