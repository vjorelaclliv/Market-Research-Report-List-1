<p><p>The microbial agricultural inoculants market is primarily segmented into soil inoculation and seed inoculation. Soil inoculation involves the application of beneficial microbes directly to the soil to enhance nutrient availability, improve soil health, and promote plant growth. Seed inoculation, on the other hand, involves coating seeds with microbial agents before planting, which helps establish beneficial relationships between the microbes and the developing plant, leading to improved germination rates and enhanced resilience against pathogens. Both methods aim to optimize agricultural productivity sustainably.</p></p>
