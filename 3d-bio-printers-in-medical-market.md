<p><p>3D bioprinters in the medical market utilize various technologies for tissue engineering and regenerative medicine. Magnetic 3D bioprinting uses magnetic fields to assemble cells and materials. Laser-assisted bioprinting employs focused laser beams to precisely deposit cells and biomaterials. Inkjet 3D bioprinting generates droplets to create fine structures layer by layer. Microextrusion 3D bioprinting extrudes bioinks through a nozzle to build larger scaffolds. Each method offers unique advantages tailored for specific applications in creating complex biological tissues.</p></p>
