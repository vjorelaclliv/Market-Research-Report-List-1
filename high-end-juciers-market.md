<p><p>The high-end juicer market is categorized into high-speed and low-speed juicers, catering to different consumer preferences. High-speed juicers, often centrifugal, quickly extract juice by spinning at high RPMs. They are efficient but may introduce heat, affecting nutrient retention. In contrast, low-speed juicers, typically masticating or triturating, operate at slower speeds to preserve nutrients and enzymes, yielding high-quality juice. This segment appeals to health-conscious consumers seeking optimal extraction and flavor, highlighting a trend towards nutritional wellness.</p></p>
