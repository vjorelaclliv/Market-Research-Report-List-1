<p><p>Reusable Satellite Launch Vehicles (RSLVs) are designed to facilitate cost-effective access to space by allowing components to be reused after flights. The market is divided into two main types: partially reusable and fully reusable. Partially reusable systems typically refurbish only certain components, such as the first stage, while fully reusable systems aim to recover and reuse the entire launch vehicle. This differentiation impacts development costs, operational efficiency, and overall affordability, driving innovation and competitive dynamics in the space industry.</p></p>
